Publications

  • Oct 2024
    Fiery hands: Designing thermal glove through thermal and tactile integration for virtual object manipulation
    ACM Symposium on User Interface Software and Technology (UIST)
    This work presents a novel method for delivering localized and global thermal feedback to the palm and fingertips while enabling unobstructed virtual object manipulation. By combining thermal and tactile actuators and leveraging perceptual phenomena like thermal referral, the approach enhances sensory immersion in VR, creating diverse and energy-efficient thermal interactions with virtual objects.
  • Oct 2024
    Wetness Illusion in Mid-Air
    IEEE International Symposium on Mixed and Augmented Reality (ISMAR)
    This study investigates the perception of wetness in mid-air by combining sensory inputs like temperature, pressure, and visual stimuli. Findings reveal that higher temperature and pressure enhance wetness perception, with visual context playing a significant role. These insights contribute to designing more immersive VR experiences that effectively simulate sensory interactions.
  • Oct 2024
    Thermal In Motion: Designing Thermal Flow Illusions with Tactile and Thermal Interaction
    ACM Symposium on User Interface Software and Technology (UIST)
    This study introduces a method to create dynamic thermal sensations by combining thermal referral with tactile motion. Through experiments on human perception, the work identifies optimal conditions for perceiving thermal motion and speed thresholds for hot and cold sensations. Integration into VR demonstrates the potential of thermal motion for enhancing immersive experiences and advancing thermal interface design.
  • Oct 2023
    Fabric Thermal Display using Ultrasonic Waves
    IEEE International Symposium on Mixed and Augmented Reality (ISMAR)
    This paper introduces a fabric-based thermal display combining polyester and thermally-conductive materials, enhanced with an ultrasound haptic display. Experiments demonstrate that integrating materials like copper and aluminum improves thermal perception and identification compared to fabric alone. The approach is applied to a VR glove, showcasing its potential for immersive and tactile-rich virtual experiences.
  • May 2024
    Thermal Masking: When the Illusion Takes Over the Real
    ACM Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems (CHI)
    This paper explores thermal masking, a phenomenon where thermal sensations are perceived only at tactile sites, effectively masking the original thermal sensation. Through experiments, the research investigates the conditions and spatial extent of this illusion, offering insights into how thermal and tactile interactions can be leveraged for immersive technologies.
  • May 2024
    Let It Snow: Designing Snowfall Experience in VR
    ACM Interactive Mobile Wearable Ubiquitous Technology (IMWUT)
    This paper introduces Snow, a cross-modal interface combining cold and tactile stimuli to create immersive VR experiences like catching snowflakes or feeling raindrops on bare hands. By integrating visual and haptic cues, the system simulates realistic weather effects, leveraging human perception to enhance the sensation of snowfall and rainfall in virtual environments.
  • Feb 2023
    Upper Body Thermal Referral and Tactile Masking for Localized Feedback
    IEEE Transactions on Visualization and Computer Graphics
    This paper explores the use of thermal referral and tactile masking to deliver localized thermal feedback on the upper body. By combining thermal and tactile sensations, the study demonstrates that precise thermal feedback can be achieved with fewer thermal actuators, enhancing response time and location accuracy compared to thermal-only setups. These findings offer valuable insights for designing efficient thermal-based wearables and immersive VR experiences.
  • Dec 2021
    Mid-Air Thermo-Tactile Feedback using Ultrasound Haptic Display
    ACM Symposium on Virtual Reality Software and Technology (VRST)
    This paper introduces a mid-air thermo-tactile feedback system using an ultrasound haptic display, combining heated airflow and focused pressure points to deliver simultaneous thermal and vibrotactile cues. The system achieves stable thermal feedback and accurate haptic pattern identification, demonstrating that thermo-tactile integration enhances user experiences in immersive applications.
  • Apr 2022
    Improving Finger Stroke Recognition Rate for Eyes-Free Mid-Air Typing in VR
    ACM Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems (CHI)
    This study analyzes mid-air typing data from touch typists to evaluate features and classifiers for recognizing finger strokes. Using motion capture data, kinematic and temporal features are assessed, and classifiers like Naive Bayes, Random Forest, and Deep Neural Networks are compared. Incorporating linguistic heuristics further enhances keystroke prediction accuracy, providing insights for improving mid-air typing systems.
